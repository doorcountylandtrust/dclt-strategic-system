# — Website CTAs & Messaging Updates

(Based on 2015 Survey Findings)

### 1. Homepage CTA & Messaging Updates

**Insight:** Members are older, highly engaged, and prioritize stewardship, conservation, and recreation.

**New CTA & Messaging Approach**

- **Before:** “Take Action to Protect Wild Places”
- **Refined:** “Protect the Land You Love – Explore, Volunteer, or Give Today”
- **Supporting Subtext:**
    
    > “From scenic hiking trails to critical land conservation, Door County’s natural areas thrive because of supporters like you. Discover how you can make a difference.”
    > 
- **Primary CTA:** “Explore Preserves” (targets hikers, sightseers)
- **Secondary CTA:** “Become a Conservation Member” (targets donors & legacy givers)

**Implementation**

- Position a clear, action-oriented CTA at the top of the homepage.
- Offer two main engagement paths: one for recreation, one for financial support.

---

### 2. Preserve Explorer Page CTA & Messaging Updates

**Insight:** Members visit preserves frequently, with strong interest in hiking, sightseeing, and birdwatching.

**New CTA & Messaging Approach**

- **Before:** “Find a Preserve”
- **Refined:** “Find Your Next Adventure – Explore Scenic Trails & Protected Lands”
- **Supporting Subtext:**
    
    > “From birdwatching at Heins Creek to scenic hikes at Kangaroo Lake, explore preserves based on your interests.”
    > 
- **Primary CTA:** “Use Interactive Map”
- **Secondary CTA:** “Plan Your Visit”

**Implementation**

- Introduce filters to personalize the user journey (e.g., hiking, family-friendly, wildlife).
- Include seasonal recommendations (e.g., “Best Fall Color Hikes”).

---

### 3. Conservation & Land Protection Messaging Updates

**Insight:** Members are deeply concerned about development pressures, habitat loss, and water quality.

**New CTA & Messaging Approach**

- **Before:** “Support Conservation”
- **Refined:** “Keep Door County Wild – Help Protect More Land”
- **Supporting Subtext:**
    
    > “Development is putting pressure on Door County’s natural landscapes. Your support ensures these lands remain protected for wildlife and future generations.”
    > 
- **Primary CTA:** “Donate to Protect Land”
- **Secondary CTA:** “See Conservation Wins”

**Implementation**

- Use urgency-driven messaging tied to current conservation threats.
- Provide concrete impact numbers (“X acres protected in 2024”).

---

### 4. Volunteer & Get Involved Messaging Updates

**Insight:** Members want more ways to get involved beyond donating—especially through volunteering and events.

**New CTA & Messaging Approach**

- **Before:** “Volunteer with Us”
- **Refined:** “Be a Steward of the Land – Volunteer or Join an Event”
- **Supporting Subtext:**
    
    > “Help restore prairies, maintain trails, or assist with conservation projects. Whether you’re looking for hands-on work or a guided nature experience, there’s a way for everyone to get involved.”
    > 
- **Primary CTA:** “Find a Volunteer Opportunity”
- **Secondary CTA:** “See Upcoming Events”

**Implementation**

- Feature more hands-on stewardship in event listings.
- Include personal volunteer stories to showcase impact.

---

### 5. Donor Engagement & Legacy Giving Updates

**Insight:** Members highly value DCLT, with 40.7% ranking it as a top charity.

**New CTA & Messaging Approach**

- **Before:** “Donate Now”
- **Refined:** “Leave a Legacy – Protect Land for Generations”
- **Supporting Subtext:**
    
    > “Your support ensures that Door County’s wild places stay protected forever. Join as a member or consider a planned gift.”
    > 
- **Primary CTA:** “Become a Member”
- **Secondary CTA:** “Learn About Legacy Giving”

**Implementation**

- Offer membership tiers with impact-focused messaging (“Your $50 gift protects X acres of wetlands”).
- Introduce legacy giving as a featured donation option on the donation page.

---

### 6. News & Updates CTA & Messaging Updates

**Insight:** Members trust DCLT’s communications and want more stewardship and science-based content.

**New CTA & Messaging Approach**

- **Before:** “Read Our Blog”
- **Refined:** “Conservation in Action – See the Latest Wins & Research”
- **Supporting Subtext:**
    
    > “From habitat restoration to conservation policy, explore the latest updates from Door County’s land protection efforts.”
    > 
- **Primary CTA:** “Read Conservation Stories”
- **Secondary CTA:** “Sign Up for Our Newsletter”

**Implementation**

- Rebrand blog/news section as a conservation hub.
- Expand stewardship-focused content (e.g., native plant restoration, water quality).
- Use impact storytelling with before-and-after conservation photos.

---

### ✅ Final Strategy Recommendations

- Refocus homepage CTAs on the two key engagement paths: **Explore Nature** vs. **Protect Land**.
- Make Preserve Explorer more interactive (filters, seasonal highlights).
- Strengthen conservation messaging with urgency and impact data.
- Increase volunteer engagement via event-based outreach.
- Improve donor messaging to highlight **legacy giving** and long-term funding impact.
- Position News/Blog as a **resource hub** for stewardship and scientific learning.
