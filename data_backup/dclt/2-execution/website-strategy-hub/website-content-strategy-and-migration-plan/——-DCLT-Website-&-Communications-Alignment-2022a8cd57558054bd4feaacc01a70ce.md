## ✅ 1. PRIORITIZED WEBSITE REDESIGN TASKS

### 🔴 High Priority

- **Protect Your Land Hub**
    - Create a unified landing page with clear CTAs: Donate, Easement, Sell
    - Remove dense paragraphs; replace with scannable cards or toggles
- **Salesforce-Integrated Forms**
    - Redesign Volunteer, Contact, and Map Request forms
    - Sync fields with Salesforce schema (use consistent labels)
- **Hunting Program UX Overhaul**
    - Improve page structure, triage email routing, and surface maps
    - Add clear "Talk to a Hunting Program Manager" CTA
- **Clarify Membership vs Donation CTAs**
    - Add “Renew Membership” button alongside “Donate”
    - Adjust homepage CTA logic; match GiveLively language
- **Build CE & Land Protection Explainers**
    - Create myth-busting FAQ for CE misconceptions
    - Design a skim-friendly page: “Ways to Protect Land” with 3 big options

### 🟡 Medium Priority

- **Preserve vs Natural Area Differentiation**
    - Add a visual explainer + access rules
    - Include “Not for hiking” icons + preserve-only filters
- **Project Archive Pages**
    - Allow Restoration/Stewardship staff to document year-over-year progress
    - Tie into funder reporting and outreach storytelling
- **Update Publications Page + Workflow**
    - Create a simple publishing pipeline with staff assignments
- **Event UX Fix**
    - Redesign event pages to highlight RSVP/tickets first
    - Add calendar view or filterable list

### 🟢 Low Priority

- **AllTrails + Mobile Access Features**
    - Link preserve pages to trail tools like AllTrails
    - Include downloadable PDFs or GPS tools where possible
- **Embed Social Posts on Website**
    - Highlight seasonal stories and call-to-action posts
- **Create Seasonal Stewardship Calendar**
    - “This month in your backyard” style blog section or downloadable content

## 🔍 4. IA REDESIGN CROSS-CHECK

### What to audit:

- Are highest-traffic tools (Donate, Hunting, Events) accessible within 1–2 clicks?
- Is “Protect Your Land” surfaced as a clear decision point?
- Are Stewardship and Land staff pages written for **external users**, not internal jargon?
- Does the site reflect **both place-based magic and people-centered storytelling**?
