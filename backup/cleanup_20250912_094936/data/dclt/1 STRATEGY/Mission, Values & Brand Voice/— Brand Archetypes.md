### **The Caregiver** - The Caregiver archetype focuses on nurturing, protecting, and creating stability for others. Itâ€™s driven by altruism and service.

Traits:

- Core Desire: To help and protect others (or the land, wildlife, and future generations in this case).
- Goal: To preserve and sustain wildlands and water for the benefit of the environment and communities.
- Strategy: Act responsibly, provide stability, and create lasting impact through care and stewardship.
- Voice: Empathetic, nurturing, protective, and grounded.

### The Explorer - The Explorer archetype is also highly relevant for land trusts, especially if your organization emphasizes adventure, discovery, and connection to wild places.

Traits:

- Core Desire: To seek freedom and explore uncharted or protected lands.
- Goal: To preserve the beauty and wildness of nature, enabling others to explore and connect with it.
- Strategy: Highlight the untamed beauty of nature, inspire action through adventure, and advocate for conservation.
- Voice: Adventurous, inspiring, bold, and forward-thinking.

### The Hero - The Hero focuses on taking bold, decisive action to solve problems and create lasting change. Itâ€™s driven by the desire to prove its strength, inspire others, and make the world a better place.

Traits:

- Core Desire: To make the world better by overcoming great challenges (e.g., saving wildlands and protecting biodiversity).
- Goal: To inspire action, demonstrate strength, and lead the charge in solving environmental issues.
- Strategy: Act decisively, demonstrate courage, and mobilize others to join the cause.
- Voice: Empowering, bold, confident, and focused on action.
