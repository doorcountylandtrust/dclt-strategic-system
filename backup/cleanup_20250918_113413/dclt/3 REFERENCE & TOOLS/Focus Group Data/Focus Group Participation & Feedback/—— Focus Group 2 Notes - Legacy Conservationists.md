# —— Focus Group 2 Notes - Legacy Conservationists

## Door County Land Trust Rebrand Research

**Date:** July 11, 2025

**Duration:** 90 minutes

**Purpose:** Gather feedback on Door County Land Trust's visual rebranding efforts and community engagement strategies

**Facilitator:** Andrew Goldsworthy

**Brand Designer:** Don Krumpos

**Land Trust Staff:** Lucia, Cinnamon

---

## Detailed Participant Profiles & Connections

### Carolyn (Real Estate Professional, Conservation Easement Holder)

**Background:** 50+ years in Door County, retired real estate professional, conservation easement on 20 acres

**Connection to Land Trust:**

- Early connection through Jim Zimmerman, Land Trust founder
- Helped with property evaluations and early land transactions
- Recently established conservation easement after reading Land Trust newsletter
- Values land protection due to development pressures witnessed over decades

**Key Quote:** *"Door County isn't all woods. The woods are fabulous... but if we lose our meadows, it's going to be quite a different county."*

### Bruce Pikas (Conservation Easement Holder, Ridges Volunteer)

**Background:** Conservation easement holder, primarily volunteers with The Ridges sanctuary

**Connection to Land Trust:**

- Purchased 40-acre property with wife to prevent development
- Created conservation easement to ensure long-term protection
- Limited direct volunteer involvement but strong mission support
- Motivated by preventing "giant houses being built in the middle of fields"

**Key Quote:** *"The more land that can be protected the better off, this county will be."*

### Carol & Bruce Olson (Former Active Volunteers)

**Background:** 25+ years with Door County, former intensive Land Trust volunteers

**Connection to Land Trust:**

- Early volunteers during "personable era" with Laurel Hauser, Terry Cooper, Lucy Klug
- Maintained Northern Kangaroo Lake area properties
- Stepped back after feeling organizational changes reduced volunteer welcome
- Continue donating but redirect active involvement to other organizations

**Key Quote:** *"It doesn't feel as warm and cuddly as it did years ago... they want our money, but I don't feel they really care about us."*

### Donna Hermann (Multi-generational Connection)

**Background:** Family cottage since 1981, now full-time resident, parents were early volunteers

**Connection to Land Trust:**

- Parents active as hike leaders and social volunteers
- Childhood exposure through family camping and hiking
- Now seeking stewardship opportunities and habitat restoration involvement
- Interested in master gardener training to support Land Trust work

**Key Quote:** *"Dad was a hike leader and mower of trails... it's been incredible work and so much land saved."*

## In-Depth Topic Analysis

### 1. Organizational Relationship & Perception - Detailed Discussion

**The "Personal Era" vs. Professional Growth:**

- **Carol's perspective:** *"25 years ago... the people that brought us in... it just seems like they were more personable. And we were all kind of a big family"*
- **Transition impact:** Multiple staff changes (development director, executive director, stewardship staff changed 2-3 times each)
- **Volunteer displacement:** *"When we moved back here... they didn't really care for volunteers, and they just really turned us off"*
- **Current engagement level:** Still donate and attend annual picnic, but energy redirected to other organizations

**Professional Evolution Challenges:**

- **Donna's mother's experience:** Role eliminated during staff expansion, felt no longer needed
- **Efficiency vs. Community:** Land Trust streamlined operations, eliminating some volunteer-driven activities
- **Scale impact:** Annual budget grew from ~$350,000 to current level with 15+ staff members
- **Lost intimacy:** Transition from "station wagon fundraising" to professional development

### 2. Land Trust Role Definition - Mission Priorities

**Land Protection as Primary Mission:**

- **Unanimous consensus** across all participants
- **Bruce's framework:** *"Protection should be number one. Obviously the land has to be taken care of... but I think that the more land that can be protected the better off this county will be"*
- **Carolyn's agreement:** *"I think protection is crucial"*
- **Donna's validation:** *"I agree, because the other groups are doing so much more of the other pieces"*

**Education Role - Adult-Focused:**

- **Carol's vision:** *"Offering classes... letting other people know, hopefully younger people, how important it is to protect this land"*
- **Suggested formats:** Classes at Northern Door, Crossroads, visitor center materials
- **Content focus:** Why land protection matters, how to care for land, adding trees to certain areas
- **Youth engagement:** Coloring books for visitor centers to "plant the seed"

**Collaboration Strategy:**

- **Donna's framework:** *"There's a lot of synergy... between all of the conservation groups up here, which is fantastic"*
- **Examples:** Festival of Nature, tree giveaways, habitat restoration workshops
- **Model inspiration:** Ice Age Trail Alliance's large group work weekends
- **Strength in overlap:** *"Economy of scale when it comes to communication, and where people can go to do things"*

### 3. Visual Brand Analysis - Comprehensive Feedback

**Mood Board Preferences - Detailed Responses:**

**Option A Reception:**

- **Carol's initial attraction:** *"A just the minute it came up... grabbed my attention right away... I like the little movement that you have in the photographs"*
- **Design appreciation:** Non-rectangular photo layouts, dynamic composition
- **Color response:** Strong reaction to color palette from artistic perspective

**Option C Dominance:**

- **Carolyn's assessment:** *"I think I like C. It just seemed a little cleaner, a little less confusing"*
- **Bruce's agreement:** *"I probably like C the best too. It's not as cluttered... it does look cleaner to me"*
- **Donna's immediate draw:** *"I was drawn to C... right to the bottom left corner... the graphic with the birds and woods and water"*
- **Visual elements appreciated:** Path in woods photo, birds/water/woods graphics

**Option B Rejection:**

- **Consistent criticism:** "Too light," doesn't make a statement
- **Color problems:** *"The colors don't relate to nature necessarily"*
- **Lack of impact:** Insufficient visual strength for Door County representation

**Font Analysis (Post-Color Discussion):**

- **Bruce's revelation:** *"Now that you pointed out, I think that the fonts in B are very good, are better than the other 2"*
- **Carolyn's consideration:** *"If the color was different, I would actually probably like the design of B"*
- **Design combination potential:** Clean fonts from B with stronger colors from A/C

### 4. Door County Representation Requirements (current logo)

**Essential Visual Elements:**

- **Water and bluffs:** *"I like the fact that in the background there are bluffs, and there's obviously water"*
- **Mixed forest composition:** *"Maybe it's a mixed woods. Maybe we need some hardwood trees and some pine trees"*
- **Meadow inclusion:** Carolyn's passionate advocacy for meadow representation

**Current Logo Assessment:**

- **Immediate reactions:** "Boring," "doesn't do anything for me," "looks outdated"
- **Specific criticism:** *"I do have a problem with whatever is going on with those trees that look like teepees"*
- **Positive elements:** Bluffs and water in background, bird inclusion, archway concept
- **Modification suggestions:** Remove confusing foreground trees, add hardwood variety, maintain "window" feeling

### 5. Content Strategy Preferences - Comprehensive Framework

**Newsletter Reception:**

- **Carolyn's positive impact:** *"Reading one last fall prompted me to think about putting conservation on my land"*
- **Donna's usage:** *"I keep it around... read through it over the course of a few days, and highlight a few things"*
- **Length concerns:** Bruce finds it too long, doesn't read completely
- **Visual appreciation:** Donna likes photo-heavy, quick-read format

**Priority Content Types:**

1. **New property acquisitions** (Bruce's primary interest)
2. **Conservation stories and history** - how properties came to protection
3. **Property characteristics and uniqueness**
4. **Progress statistics and protection milestones**

**Historical Storytelling (Bruce's detailed framework):**

- **Property background:** *"A little bit of the history of who it belonged to, and what it looks like"*
- **Visual documentation:** *"Photographs, if possible, as to what the property looks like or what it consists of"*
- **Unique features:** Special characteristics that make each property significant
- **Geographic education:** Learning about southern Door County properties through descriptions

### 6. Communication Preferences - Format Analysis

**Print Preference Strength:**

- **Bruce's rationale:** *"I prefer mail. Only because I need something, and it's nice to be able to hold on to it, and look at it rather than looking at on the screen"*
- **Sharing capacity:** *"Also be able to show other people if they're visiting"*
- **Reference value:** Donna keeps newsletters for extended periods
- **Physical impact:** Tangible materials feel more substantial and permanent

**Calendar Enthusiasm:**

- **Donna's kitchen counter placement:** Regular daily reference and month-by-month engagement
- **Phenology appreciation:** *"It's fun to know that today... the skunk babies are coming out"*
- **Climate tracking:** *"With climate change and things, either going earlier or whatever"*
- **Continued support:** Strong consensus for calendar continuation

**Digital Engagement Limited:**

- **Carol's avoidance:** *"I don't do Facebook. So no. I try to keep my life very simple"*
- **Generational divide recognition:** *"Maybe the young... the next generation... That's different"*
- **Strategic acceptance:** Acknowledge importance for younger demographics while maintaining print focus

### 7. Educational Content Vision

**Meadow Conservation Education (Carolyn's Passionate Advocacy):**

- **Personal experience:** *"I literally one year... pulled 500 to 800 seedlings in our meadow"*
- **Systemic observation:** *"I watch it all the time within 10. Within 20 years you have a young woods"*
- **Educational need:** *"I wish there was a little more education with the public, realizing that Door County isn't all woods"*
- **Long-term concern:** *"Nobody's going to go in and cut those trees down when they should"*

**Development Pressure Education:**

- **Carolyn's real estate perspective:** *"A lot of people up here who had farms or big pieces for generations. They have no idea the value now. And they will be taken advantage of"*
- **Marketing pressure evidence:** *"I get usually about 3 or 4 letters a week offering to... sell it, you know the 10 acres"*
- **Character change threat:** *"It'll change the character of the county that really is frightening to me"*

### 8. Volunteer Program Enhancement Needs

**Large Group Event Model (Donna's Ice Age Trail Inspiration):**

- **Scale example:** *"They will pull people from all over the State. For example, when they rebuilt the steps... at Potawatomi"*
- **Personal connection benefit:** *"That would get us back into that like personal connection again, coming together to do something big"*
- **Collaborative opportunity:** *"Perhaps with the Land Trust. There could be more of that"*

**Rotation and Inclusion:**

- **Current limitation:** Turning volunteers away due to capacity constraints
- **Proposed solution:** Conscious effort to rotate volunteers at different events
- **Engagement maintenance:** Avoid always calling same group for same activities
- **Capacity building:** Potential volunteer coordinator role to manage larger volunteer base

### 9. Community Connection Strategies

**Event Importance Recognition:**

- **Staff presence value:** Personal connections between staff and community members at events
- **Relationship building:** Individual conversations and story sharing
- **Mission authenticity:** Demonstrating genuine staff care for community and mission
- **Personal touch maintenance:** Balancing professional growth with grassroots feeling

**30th Anniversary Opportunity:**

- **"Celebrating Open Spaces" revival:** Historical dinner party fundraising format
- **Community table conversations:** Multiple small gatherings discussing land trust work
- **Revenue potential:** Previous peak of $32,000, but high community engagement value
- **Relationship building:** Quantifiable community impact through personal connections

## Strategic Implications & Action Framework

### **1. Visual Brand Direction: Layered Insights**

### **Refined Mood Board Strategy:**

**"Clean Heritage with Modern Accessibility"**

**What Worked from Analysis:**

- **Option C's cleanliness** universally appealed across age demographics
- **Option B's typography** appreciated once color bias removed
- **Option A's movement and authenticity** resonated with artistic perspectives
- **Door County specificity** essential (water, bluffs, mixed forests, meadows)

**Hybrid System Recommendations:**

- **Primary Brand (Public-Facing):** Clean aesthetic from C with warmer colors
- **Typography Pairing:** Modern fonts from B for accessibility and readability
- **Heritage Elements:** Selective use of A's authentic, hand-drawn feeling for donor/legacy communications
- **Local Imagery:** Authentic Door County landscapes (remove mountains, add meadows)

### **2. Communication Strategy Overhaul**

### **Print-Digital Balance:**

- **Maintain print primacy** for Legacy Conservationist demographic
- **Newsletter optimization:** Shorter, more visual, story-focused content
- **Calendar continuation:** High-value, well-received annual publication
- **Digital layering:** Supplement rather than replace print communications

### **Content Prioritization Framework:**

1. **New property acquisitions** with detailed stories and visuals
2. **Conservation impact stories** featuring landowner testimonials
3. **Property characteristics** with unique feature highlights
4. **Educational content** about meadow conservation and ecosystem management

### **3. Relationship Rebuilding Strategy**

### **Volunteer Engagement Renaissance:**

- **Large group events** modeled after Ice Age Trail Alliance approach
- **Volunteer rotation system** to include more community members
- **Personal staff connections** prioritized at all community events
- **Meaningful roles** that complement rather than compete with staff efficiency

### **Community Connection Restoration:**

- **30th anniversary programming** featuring throwback community engagement formats
- **Personal outreach** from leadership to formerly active volunteers
- **"Celebrating Open Spaces" revival** for relationship-building fundraising
- **Staff presence** at community events for personal relationship maintenance

### **4. Implementation                                Priorities**

### **Immediate Actions (0-3 months):**

- [ ]  Design mood board combining C's cleanliness with B's fonts and natural colors
- [ ]  Contact formerly active volunteers for personal reconnection conversations
- [ ]  Plan 30th anniversary throwback events for 2025
- [ ]  Redesign newsletter format for shorter, more visual storytelling

### **Medium-term Strategy (3-12 months):**

- [ ]  Implement volunteer rotation system and larger group events
- [ ]  Develop landowner story collection and presentation process
- [ ]  Create meadow conservation educational campaign
- [ ]  Establish regular staff presence at community events

### **Long-term Vision (1-3 years):**

- [ ]  Develop comprehensive donor stewardship program balancing efficiency with personal touch
- [ ]  Create educational programming addressing development pressure and conservation solutions
- [ ]  Establish volunteer coordinator role or enhanced volunteer management system

## Conclusion: Bridging Professional Growth with Community Heart

This Legacy Conservationist focus group reveals a community segment deeply committed to Door County Land Trust's mission but feeling disconnected from its organizational evolution. The path forward requires honoring their foundational contributions while building bridges to contemporary conservation needs.

**Core Challenge:** Maintaining the "warm and cuddly" community feeling within a professional, efficient organization.

**Primary Opportunity:** The 40th anniversary provides a perfect moment to celebrate legacy relationships while establishing refreshed engagement approaches.

**Brand Direction:** Clean, modern aesthetic with authentic Door County representation and accessible community voice.

**Success Foundation:** Personal relationships, meaningful volunteer opportunities, and authentic storytelling about land protection impact and community stewardship.
