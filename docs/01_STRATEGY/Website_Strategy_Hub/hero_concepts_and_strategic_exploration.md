# Hero Concepts and Strategic Exploration

This document outlines creative concepts for the Door County Land Trust website hero section. Each concept is grounded in the organization's mission and values, with a focus on emotional resonance, engagement, and storytelling. The goal is to explore innovative formats that go beyond common trends while staying aligned with donor psychology, land protection goals, and DCLT's long-term brand system.

---

## Strategic Purpose of the Hero Section

- Evoke reverence and emotional connection to the land
- Create a sense of place, belonging, and legacy
- Frame the visitor as a protector or steward
- Set the tone for the rest of the site (serene, serious, elevated)
- Invite action without pressure

---

## Trends to Be Careful About

| Trend              | Pros                    | Risks                                                      |
| ------------------ | ----------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| Video background   | Immersive, eye-catching | Can feel commercial or stock-like; performance issues      |
| Scroll parallax    | Visual interest         | Can feel gimmicky or dated; alignment bugs                 |
| SPAs / Long scroll | Narrative potential     | Hard to navigate; not mobile-friendly unless well-executed |
| Hover-based motion | Quick feedback          | Not accessible or meaningful                               |

##  Innovative Concepts for the Hero Area

### 1. **Hero That Breathes**
A visual design where elements expand and contract slowly, mimicking breath.
- Text pulses gently
- Background subtly brightens/darkens
- Nature has a pulse

**Goal:** Convey that protected land is alive, and the visitor has a role in keeping it that way

### 3. **Legacy Clock / TimeLayer Hero**
An interactive before/after/future visual that shows how the land has changed and will change.
- 3-frame slider or parallax scene
- Real data from land files (acquisition year, restoration year, future goals)

**Goal:** Reinforce long-term impact and legacy giving

### 6. **Scroll-Driven Illustrated Storyboard**
