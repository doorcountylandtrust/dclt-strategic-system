<span style="font-size: 14pt;"><strong><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-5153" src="https://www.doorcountylandtrust.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/04/Seasonal-Land-Stewardship-Assistant.jpg" alt="Seasonal-Land-Stewardship-Assistant" width="780" height="444" /></strong></span>

<span style="font-size: 14pt;">Door County Land Trust (DCLT) is seeking an individual to fill an intern/seasonal position related to fee-land stewardship. This is a unique opportunity to increase your knowledge and enhance your skillset while being inspired by the beauty of the Door County Peninsula. </span><!--more-->

<span style="font-size: 14pt;"><span style="font-size: 16pt;"><b>Primary Focus: </b></span></span><span style="font-size: 14pt;">Land Stewardship</span>

<span style="font-size: 14pt;"><span style="font-size: 16pt;"><b>Position Description: </b></span>The Land Stewardship Assistant will assist the Stewardship and Restoration Manager in implementing land stewardship activities on DCLT nature preserves and natural areas. </span>

<span style="font-size: 18pt;"><b>Responsibilities:</b></span>

<span style="font-size: 14pt;">The successful applicant will work with the Stewardship and Restoration Manager to monitor fee-land properties and assist in implementing the annual land stewardship work plan. Other duties and responsibilities include but are not limited to:</span>
<ul>
 	<li><span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: 'quattrocento sans';">Locate and mark property boundaries and install necessary signage;</span></li>
 	<li><span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: 'quattrocento sans';">Facilitate small scale restoration plans with volunteers including mapping and controlling invasive species;</span></li>
 	<li><span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: 'quattrocento sans';">Supervise volunteers at DCLT fee-land stewardship work days;</span></li>
 	<li><span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: 'quattrocento sans';">Inventory, organize, procure, and maintain stewardship supplies and equipment;</span></li>
 	<li><span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: 'quattrocento sans';">Coordinate volunteers to maintain and manage trail systems and maintain preserve infrastructure; and</span></li>
 	<li><span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: 'quattrocento sans';">Ensure collection of accurate monitoring and research data.</span></li>
</ul>
<span style="font-size: 14pt;"><span style="font-size: 16pt;"><b>Location:</b></span> The position will be based out of the Door County Land Trust office in Sturgeon Bay, Wisconsin. The position will be approximately 75% field and 25% office work.</span>

<span style="font-size: 14pt;"><span style="font-size: 16pt;"><b>Start Date</b>:</span> June 3, 2019</span>

<span style="font-size: 14pt;"><span style="font-size: 16pt;"><b>End Date</b>:</span>  October 31, 2019</span>

<span style="font-size: 14pt;">Start and end dates may be adjusted based on candidate’s availability and experience.</span>

<span style="font-size: 14pt;"><span style="font-size: 16pt;"><b>Hours per week</b>:</span> 35</span>

<span style="font-size: 14pt;"><span style="font-size: 16pt;"><b>Compensation:</b></span> $12 - $15/ hour based on experience</span>

<span style="font-size: 18pt;"><b>Minimum Qualifications:</b></span>
<ul>
 	<li><span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: 'quattrocento sans';">High school diploma or GED and 1 year’s science-related field or in land management</span></li>
 	<li><span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: 'quattrocento sans';">Experience operating and maintaining various types of equipment in a safe and efficient manner (e.g. lawn mower, chainsaw, string trimmer or similar equipment)</span></li>
 	<li><span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: 'quattrocento sans';">Must be comfortable working in variable weather conditions, under physically demanding circumstances and/or at remote locations</span></li>
 	<li><span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: 'quattrocento sans';">Must have strong written and verbal communication skills</span></li>
 	<li><span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: 'quattrocento sans';">Must have experience using GPS and GIS software</span></li>
 	<li><span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: 'quattrocento sans';">Must be able to obtain related licenses or certifications as required (e.g. First Aid, CPR, herbicide application, and driver’s license)</span></li>
</ul>
<span style="font-size: 18pt;"><b>
Preferred Qualifications:</b></span>
<ul>
 	<li><span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: 'quattrocento sans';">B.S. Degree or progress towards a B.S. Degree in a biological sciences, land management planning or related field</span></li>
 	<li><span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: 'quattrocento sans';">Working knowledge of natural systems in Wisconsin;</span></li>
 	<li><span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: 'quattrocento sans';">Knowledge of current trends and practices in conservation, land management and natural resource preservation</span></li>
 	<li><span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: 'quattrocento sans';">Ability to function productively as a member or leader of a work team</span></li>
 	<li><span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: 'quattrocento sans';">Ability to recognize plant and animal species in Wisconsin</span></li>
 	<li><span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: 'quattrocento sans';">Ability to identify invasive plant species common to Wisconsin and experience with control and monitoring projects</span></li>
 	<li><span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: 'quattrocento sans';">Experience working with volunteers and/or leading volunteer work activities</span></li>
 	<li><span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: 'quattrocento sans';">Ability to contribute to a collaborative working environment in support of the DCLT mission</span></li>
</ul>
<span style="font-size: 18pt;"><b>About the Door County Land Trust:</b></span>

<span style="font-size: 14pt;">The Door County Land Trust is a local, non-profit 501(c)3 conservation organization working to protect Door County’s exceptional lands and waters forever. The Land Trust has protected more than 8,000 acres thanks to contributions of support from members.  Door County Land Trust nature preserves are open to the public at no charge for hiking, cross-country skiing, wildlife observation, some forms of hunting, and other low-impact, non-motorized recreational activities.</span>
