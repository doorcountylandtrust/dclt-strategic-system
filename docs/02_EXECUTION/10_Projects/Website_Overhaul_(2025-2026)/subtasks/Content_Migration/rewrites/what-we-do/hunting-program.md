# Hunting on DCLT Preserves

Door County Land Trust allows hunting on select preserves as part of our commitment to ecological balance, community access, and land stewardship.

When practiced ethically and within DCLT’s guidelines, hunting plays an important role in maintaining healthy habitats.

---

## When Hunting Is Allowed

- **Dates:** Hunting is only allowed during state-approved seasons
- **Times:** Dawn to dusk only (no overnight stays or blinds)
- **Seasons:** Archery, firearm, and muzzleloader seasons are observed with additional restrictions

**Check DNR Season Dates → [Link to WI DNR](https://dnr.wisconsin.gov/)**  
**DCLT-Specific Closures → Posted at Preserve Entrances**

## Rules & Ethics

Respect is non-negotiable — for land, wildlife, and fellow users.

### DCLT-Specific Rules:
- No baiting or trapping
- No tree stands or screw-in steps
- No motorized vehicles
- Respect marked boundaries and stay on foot
- Leave no trace: pack out everything
- Hunters must carry their DCLT hunting permit

### Safety Best Practices:
- Wear blaze orange, even when not required
- Use caution near trailheads and parking areas
- Alert DCLT staff if you encounter any issues

---

## For Hikers & Non-Hunters

Hunting season is active on select preserves from **late September through early January**.

To stay safe and respectful:

- Hike during midday hours when hunting is least active
- Wear bright clothing, especially orange or pink
- Stick to marked trails and stay alert

Preserves **without** hunting include:
- Sturgeon Bay Ship Canal Nature Preserve
- Lautenbach Woods Nature Preserve

## FAQs

### Can I hunt with friends or family?
Yes, but each hunter must apply individually and be approved. Group hunts must be coordinated with DCLT.

### What if I see someone violating the rules?
Report immediately to DCLT using our [incident form](/forms/report-incident) or call our main office.

### Can I scout the land before hunting season?
Yes—non-weapon scouting is encouraged. Please avoid sensitive areas and nesting seasons.

### Are tree stands or blinds allowed?
No permanent or screw-in fixtures. Ground blinds must be temporary and removed daily.
