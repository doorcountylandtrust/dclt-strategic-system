<span style="font-size: 14pt;"><b>Location:</b> Bay Shore Blufflands Nature Preserve, 5454 Bay Shore Drive
</span>
<span style="font-size: 14pt;"><b>Date:</b> Monday, June 24, 2019
</span>
<span style="font-size: 14pt;"><b>Time:</b> 9:30-11:30
</span>
<span style="font-size: 14pt;"><b>What to wear:</b> Dress in layers for a variety of weather possibilities. Long sleeves and long pants are recommended.
</span>
<span style="font-size: 14pt;"><b>What to bring:</b> Water bottle, sunscreen, snacks, insect repellent. You may bring work gloves, goggles, kneepads or other personal gear if desired.</span>

<em><span style="font-size: 14pt;">What should I plant to attract pollinators? Which native plants will work best in my yard? </span></em>
<span style="font-size: 14pt;">Find out the answers to these questions and more during our Bay Shore Blufflands Demonstration Garden Work Day!</span>

<span style="font-size: 14pt;">We’re looking for volunteers to help with the installation and planting of our native demonstration garden—while learning best-practices in the field. Volunteers will help prepare the ground, lay out plants following a braided-row design, and learn from fellow Bay Shore Blufflands neighbors, Dan Collins and Nancy Aten. Dan and Nancy are owners and designers of Landscapes of Place, focusing on native plantings and landscape restoration.</span>

<span style="font-size: 14pt;"><em>“Demonstration gardens are a great way to socialize the ideas of connected Door County habitat and caring for conservation lands. They can also provide some ideas for landscaping after buckthorn removal.”</em>
- Dan Collins and Nancy Aten, Landscapes of Place</span>

<b><span style="font-size: 14pt;">Pollinator Gardens:</span></b>
<ul>
 	<li><span style="font-family: 'quattrocento sans'; font-size: 14pt;">Provide both food and cover for pollinators such as the monarch butterfly</span></li>
 	<li><span style="font-family: 'quattrocento sans'; font-size: 14pt;">Provide native plant species which bloom over the entire growing season</span></li>
 	<li><span style="font-family: 'quattrocento sans'; font-size: 14pt;">Aesthetically pleasing and interesting to the human eye</span></li>
 	<li><span style="font-family: 'quattrocento sans'; font-size: 14pt;">Add to human interest and provide landscaping opportunities after invasive species control</span></li>
</ul>
<b><span style="font-size: 14pt;">Braided Row Planting:</span></b>
<ul>
 	<li><span style="font-family: 'quattrocento sans'; font-size: 14pt;">A braided row of two or three shrub species in-planted with native grasses and forbs to replace buckthorn and other hedgerows consisting of invasive species</span></li>
 	<li><span style="font-family: 'quattrocento sans'; font-size: 14pt;">Enhances habitat value by providing cover and food for pollinators, birds and other wildlife because it replaces buckthorn and other invasive species while still providing privacy from neighbors</span></li>
</ul>
