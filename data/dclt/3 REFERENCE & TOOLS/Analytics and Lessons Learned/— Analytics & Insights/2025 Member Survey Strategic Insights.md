# Door County Land Trust Survey Analysis

## Executive Summary

The Door County Land Trust survey received **639 responses**, revealing a highly engaged, older, and deeply committed supporter base. The survey shows strong satisfaction with communications (96.6% find them relevant) and demonstrates significant financial commitment, with 87% providing financial support and 56% ranking DCLT in their top 5 charitable priorities.

**Key Findings:**

- **Demographic Profile**: Predominantly older (72% age 65+), retired (72%), with deep family connections to Door County
- **High Engagement**: 81% visit nature preserves multiple times per year; 87% provided financial support
- **Strong Local Ties**: 62% live in Door County year-round or seasonally; 69% have 25+ years of family connection
- **Communication Success**: 97% find communications relevant; overwhelming interest in science and stewardship content

### Sample Environmental Concerns:

- "Protect natural habitats for future generations"
- "Bird habitats, wetland preservation, erosion of the shoreline, and invasive species"
- "Spreading of high concentration of fertilizer including liquid manure has to stop"
- "State and federal govt are stepping back from environmental issues"
- "Overuse of the land. Overcrowding. Concern about monitoring of Pebble Beach"
- "Deer control and invasive species"

---

## Key Insights & Recommendations

### Strengths

1. **Highly Engaged Base**: 87% provide financial support, 81% visit preserves regularly
2. **Strong Communication**: 97% find communications relevant
3. **Deep Community Roots**: 69% have 25+ years of family connection to Door County
4. **Diversified Support**: Strong interest across multiple activity types and conservation topics

### Opportunities

1. **Younger Demographics**: Only 10% under age 55 - consider targeted outreach to younger families
2. **Volunteer Engagement**: Only 12% volunteered this year despite high interest in the mission
3. **Educational Programming**: Strong interest in science topics suggests appetite for expanded educational content
4. **Geographic Expansion**: 10% are occasional visitors who could potentially increase engagement

### Strategic Implications

- **Content Strategy**: Prioritize science-based content and stewardship stories in communications
- **Volunteer Development**: Significant opportunity to convert financial supporters to active volunteers
- **Succession Planning**: Need to cultivate younger supporters to sustain long-term mission
- **Activity Programming**: High hiking and birding participation suggests these are key engagement vehicles
