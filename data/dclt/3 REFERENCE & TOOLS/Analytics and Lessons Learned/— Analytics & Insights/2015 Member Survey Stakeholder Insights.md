# 2015 Member Survey: Stakeholder Insights

Date: August 1, 2015
Donations Generated: Insight-only survey (no direct donations)
Engagement Rate: 84% financially engaged, 20% volunteer
Lessons Learned: DCLT members are older, retired, and conservation-minded. Top values include land protection, water quality, and stewardship. Messaging should emphasize measurable impact, legacy giving, and passive engagement opportunities.

1. Member Demographics & Engagement
Insight: DCLT’s audience is predominantly older, retired, and deeply engaged in conservation.
Key Takeaways:
55% of members are 65+ years old, and 56.1% are retired.
Highly engaged group: 84% provide financial support, and nearly 20% volunteer.
Top activities: Hiking (85.7%), sightseeing (67.4%), birdwatching (52.6%), followed by bicycling and snowshoeing.
Marketing Implications:
Emphasize stewardship messaging (land conservation, legacy giving).
Tailor outreach for an older demographic with accessibility-focused activities.
Highlight passive engagement opportunities (e.g., guided tours, scenic visits) alongside active involvement.
Position preserves as wellness destinations for relaxation, nature therapy, and lifelong learning.
2. Conservation & Land Protection Priorities
Insight: Development pressure, habitat quality, and water protection are top concerns.
Key Takeaways:
60.6% worry about development pressures impacting natural landscapes.
51.1% rank habitat quality as a priority for conservation.
38.6% see water quality as a major issue.
Marketing Implications:
Showcase conservation wins with measurable impact (e.g., "We protected X acres from development").
Use storytelling to highlight how land preservation directly supports wildlife and local water quality.
Leverage advocacy messaging—position DCLT as a trusted voice on land-use policies and conservation challenges.
3. Financial & Donor Engagement
Insight: Members strongly value DCLT’s work and prioritize it in charitable giving.
Key Takeaways:
40.7% consider DCLT a top 5 charity, and 31.1% place it in their top 10.
5.6% named DCLT their primary nonprofit.
Marketing Implications:
Strengthen donor messaging with clear impact stories:
"Your support protected 1,000 acres of vital wetlands in 2023!"
Promote planned giving & legacy donations to deepen long-term donor commitment.
Leverage peer influence: Feature member testimonials explaining why they support DCLT.
4. Communication & Content Preferences
Insight: Members trust DCLT’s communications and want more land stewardship & science-based content.
Key Takeaways:
86% find DCLT’s communications informative.
Top interests:
Land stewardship & restoration (71%)
Science & ecology topics (65.9%)
Recreation & educational content (52.9%)
Marketing Implications:
Expand science-based storytelling (e.g., “How Protected Wetlands Improve Water Quality”).
Offer more “behind-the-scenes” stewardship updates via blog posts, email, and social media.
Personalize content: Create custom email series based on member interests (e.g., conservation policy, outdoor recreation, or restoration projects).
Improve content discoverability: Feature seasonal recreation guides (“Best Fall Hikes,” “Where to See Spring Wildflowers”) to drive engagement.
5. Areas for Growth
Insight: Members want more land protection, better stewardship, and expanded recreation opportunities.
Key Takeaways:
24.1% want DCLT to acquire more land.
21% prioritize land stewardship & restoration.
13.7% want more outdoor recreation options.
13.1% seek improved events & outings.
Marketing Implications:
Align fundraising asks with these priorities (e.g., “Help Us Protect 500 More Acres This Year”).
Create new ways to engage members outdoors, such as:
Preserve ambassador program (volunteer-led hikes).
Citizen science initiatives (bird counts, native plant monitoring).
Enhance event offerings:
More educational hikes & workshops for hands-on engagement.
Exclusive member appreciation events to deepen connections.
Final Strategic Takeaways
Older, engaged members drive conservation efforts. Tailor messaging and programs to their interests.
Land protection and water quality resonate most. Highlight these themes in campaigns.
Donors see DCLT as a top-tier charity. Strengthen fundraising with storytelling and impact metrics.
Members want more recreation & stewardship content. Expand blog, email, and event programming.
Conservation storytelling should be a core strategy. Use visuals, testimonials, and data to reinforce your mission.
