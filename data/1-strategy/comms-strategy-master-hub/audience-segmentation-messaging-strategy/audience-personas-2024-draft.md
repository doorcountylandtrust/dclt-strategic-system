# —— Audience Personas – 2024 Draft

These personas represent our current understanding of DCLT audience segments based on survey insights, behavior analysis, and staff experience. They will be revisited and refined as part of the Audience Data Task Force work in Spring 2025.

### Persona 1: Engaged Donor Ella

**Demographics**:

- Age Range: 65+
- Gender: Any
- Location: Primarily Door County, some seasonal residents from the Midwest
- Employment/Occupation: Retired professionals, former educators, conservation enthusiasts
- Household Income: Middle to upper-income levels

**Psychographics**:

- Interests: Hiking, birdwatching, environmental conservation, legacy giving
- Values: Protecting natural landscapes, long-term conservation impact, ensuring land remains undeveloped
- Goals: Support tangible conservation projects, see measurable impact of their contributions
- Challenges: Unsure how donations are utilized beyond general conservation efforts, prefers low-tech engagement

**Engagement Preferences:**

- Preferred Communication Channels: Print newsletters, email updates, in-person donor events
- Most Engaging Content Types: Conservation success stories, donor impact reports, planned giving case studies
- Participation: Makes annual donations, may attend special donor recognition events

**Behavior Patterns:**

- Giving Behavior: Consistently contributes, often includes DCLT in top 5 charities, may consider legacy giving
- Event Attendance: Attends appreciation events, stewardship talks, and major conservation announcements
- Digital Behavior: Limited social media use, prefers traditional channels

**Opportunities for Engagement:**

- What inspires them to act? Seeing the direct impact of donations through conservation statistics and personal stories
- How can we better engage them? More personalized donor updates, legacy gift planning workshops, behind-the-scenes tours

### Persona 2: Outdoor Enthusiast Olivia

**Demographics**:

- Age Range: 45-64
- Gender: Any
- Location: Door County and regional Midwest visitors
- Employment/Occupation: Working professionals, eco-conscious travelers, educators
- Household Income: Middle to upper-middle income

**Psychographics**:

- Interests: Hiking, biking, birdwatching, sustainable travel
- Values: Outdoor recreation, environmental education, responsible tourism
- Goals: Find accessible and well-maintained preserves, engage with nature sustainably
- Challenges: May not understand how DCLT operates beyond maintaining trails, unaware of conservation threats

**Engagement Preferences:**

- Preferred Communication Channels: Email, social media (Instagram, Facebook), interactive maps
- Most Engaging Content Types: Seasonal hiking guides, conservation storytelling, species highlights
- Participation: Visits preserves regularly but not highly engaged in giving

**Behavior Patterns:**

- Giving Behavior: May contribute to specific campaigns, less likely to be recurring donors
- Event Attendance: Occasionally attends hikes or workshops but not donor-focused events
- Digital Behavior: Engages with DCLT via social media and preserve pages, but doesn’t frequent donation pages

**Opportunities for Engagement:**

- What inspires them to act? Clear connections between recreation and conservation impact
- How can we better engage them? Develop more guided hike programs, promote adopt-a-trail programs, integrate donation CTAs into hike planning tools

### Persona 3: Conservation Volunteer Victor

Demographics:

- Age Range: 25-44
- Gender: Any
- Location: Regional Wisconsin, Door County visitors
- Employment/Occupation: Early/mid-career professionals, students, conservation workers
- Household Income: Lower to middle income

**Psychographics**:

- Interests: Sustainability, hands-on environmental work, community involvement
- Values: Active land stewardship, habitat restoration, local conservation impact
- Goals: Contribute time instead of just money, learn conservation skills
- Challenges: Limited availability for in-person events, may not see long-term impact of volunteering

**Engagement Preferences:**

- Preferred Communication Channels: Social media, email, text notifications for volunteer shifts
- Most Engaging Content Types: Action-oriented volunteer stories, how-to conservation videos, restoration impact updates
- Participation: Regular volunteer, occasional donor

**Behavior Patterns:**

- Giving Behavior: More likely to donate if tied to a volunteer effort (e.g., “Support the trail you helped restore”)
- Event Attendance: Participates in hands-on stewardship events, less likely to attend fundraising galas
- Digital Behavior: Engages via Instagram and event RSVPs, likely to share DCLT posts

**Opportunities for Engagement:**

- What inspires them to act? Seeing real impact from volunteer efforts, clear calls to action
- How can we better engage them? Offer micro-volunteering opportunities, gamify conservation efforts (volunteer challenges)

### Persona 4: Legacy Giver Linda

**Demographics**:

- Age Range: 65+
- Gender: Any
- Location: Door County or seasonal resident
- Employment/Occupation: Retired, estate planning-minded donors
- Household Income: Upper income, financially stable

**Psychographics**:

- Interests: Philanthropy, estate planning, ensuring long-term impact
- Values: Preservation beyond their lifetime, protecting Door County’s character
- Goals: Leave a lasting impact through legacy gifts, ensure land stays protected forever
- Challenges: May not fully understand the planned giving process, hesitant about financial commitment

**Engagement Preferences:**

- Preferred Communication Channels: Personalized mailings, estate planning workshops, one-on-one donor meetings
- Most Engaging Content Types: Legacy donor testimonials, financial impact reports, estate planning guides
- Participation: Long-term donor, occasional event attendee

**Behavior Patterns:**

- Giving Behavior: More likely to engage in estate planning conversations than immediate donations
- Event Attendance: Prefers intimate, small-group donor events or expert-led estate planning sessions
- Digital Behavior: Uses email but prefers personal outreach over mass communications

**Opportunities for Engagement:**

- What inspires them to act? Understanding the lasting impact of a planned gift
- How can we better engage them? One-on-one donor stewardship, personalized impact stories, planned giving seminars
